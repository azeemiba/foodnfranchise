Sindhi biryani

It is a popular dish from the Sindh province of Pakistan. It is a savory dish made with layers of marinated meat, fragrant basmati rice, and a combination of spices such as cumin, coriander, and garam masala. It is a spicy and flavorful dish that often includes potatoes, tomatoes, and chopped green chilies. It is typically served with raita, a yogurt-based side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups Basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 600 grams beef/mutton, cut into small pieces
  • cup yogurt
  • 1 cup fried onions
  • cup tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • tbsp ginger paste
  • tbsp garlic paste
  • tbsp Sindhi biryani masala (available at any grocery store)
  • tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • tsp coriander powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon powder
  • 1/4 tsp cloves powder
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg powder
  • A pinch of saffron (optional)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • green chilies, sliced
  • 4-5 dried plums (optional)
  • 2 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • 2 cup mint leaves, chopped

Directions:

  1. In a deep pot or pressure cooker, heat oil and add cumin seeds.
  2. the seeds crackle, add ginger and garlic paste and fry for a minute.
  3. meat and fry for 2-3 minutes until it changes color.
  4. Sindhi biryani masala, salt, black pepper, coriander powder, turmeric, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg powder. Mix well.
  5. Add yogurt, fried onions, and tomatoes. Mix well and cook until tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Add lemon juice, saffron, green chilies, and dried plums. Mix well.
  7. Drain the rice and add it to the pot or pressure cooker. Add enough water to cook the rice.
  8. Once the rice is cooked, sprinkle coriander leaves and mint leaves over the top.
  9. hot with raita and salad.
